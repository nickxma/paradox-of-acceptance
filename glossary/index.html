Acceptance Coming soon
Meeting what arises without adding resistance — and the question of whether that means changing it or not.
Attention Coming soon
The directed, trainable faculty that chooses what to notice — distinguished from awareness itself.
Awareness
The capacity to know experience directly — the ground in which perception, thought, and sensation arise. Radically different depending on whether you are cultivating it or recognizing it.
E
Equanimity Coming soon
Stability in the face of changing conditions — and the fine line between genuine groundedness and sophisticated avoidance.
I
Impermanence Coming soon
The first characteristic of existence in Buddhist frameworks: nothing lasts, including the sense of a fixed self.
N
Non-self Coming soon
The claim — and the experience — that the bounded self one assumed oneself to be is not what it appeared to be.
P
Presence Coming soon
Being here rather than lost in thought — and how this differs at relative versus ultimate levels of analysis.
S
Samadhi Coming soon
Meditative concentration or unification of mind — ranging from relaxed focus to absorption states that suspend ordinary experience.
Suffering (Dukkha) Coming soon
The pervasive unsatisfactoriness the Buddha identified — from obvious pain to the subtle dis-ease of conditioned existence.
T
Two Truths Coming soon
The framework distinguishing relative (conventional) reality from ultimate reality — and why both are needed to avoid confusion.
