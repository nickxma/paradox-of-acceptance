Dosage: How Much Meditation Is Too Much?

March 10, 2026 · 41 min

Show Notes

There's no universal answer to how much you should meditate. But there are useful heuristics, and there are warning signs at both ends of the spectrum that most teachers don't discuss.

What We Cover

  • The research on minimum effective dose (it's lower than most apps suggest)
  • Signs you're under-practicing: the "insight lag" problem
  • Signs you're over-practicing: destabilization, depersonalization, what to do
  • How to design a practice schedule that fits a real life
  • The retreat question: when do intensive periods help vs. when do they destabilize?

The Destabilization Curve

More practice isn't always better. There's a curve. Early on, more practice accelerates clarity. At some point — different for everyone — it produces more disruption than integration can handle. Learning to recognize where you are on that curve is a core skill.

The signs you've crossed the threshold: persistent anxiety that meditation doesn't relieve, dissociation during daily activities, difficulty holding ordinary attention on tasks, emotional flooding without a clear trigger. These aren't signs of failure. They're signs to back off and integrate.

Practical Heuristics

  1. Can you function well and notice the effects of practice in daily life? You're probably in range.
  2. Are you consistently agitated, anxious, or dissociated after sessions? Back off.
  3. Are you barely noticing any effect? You might need more — or a different technique.
