Nick
Nick writes about the questions that serious meditation practice raises — the strange structural tensions, the beliefs that quietly dissolve, and the things that get harder before they get easier. He's been practicing for over a decade, and has spent much of that time trying to understand what the tradition doesn't say out loud. Paradox of Acceptance is where that thinking lives: essays, tools, and guided practices for people who want to go deeper than the wellness framing usually allows.
What you'll receive
  • Essay drops — long-form writing on what mindfulness actually does to a person. No wellness platitudes. The weird stuff, the hard stuff, the stuff that changes how you see.
  • Contemplative practice prompts — short, pointed invitations to look for yourself. Not guided meditations. Questions that don't resolve neatly.
  • Mindfulness-meets-technology lens — honest writing about what it means to practice in a world shaped by attention engineering, optimization culture, and systems designed to keep you reactive.
